Al Mokawloon (2010-2012)

Salah began his football career at Ittihad Basyoun and Othman Tanta clubs, before joining the Arab Contractors Club as a youth in 2006, which was his first creative station. Mohamed Salah played his first match in the Egyptian Premier League on May 3, 2010, when he was eighteen years old, against Mansoura Club in a match that ended in a 1-1 draw, and Salah participated as a substitute in this match. In the 2010-11 season, Salah became a key player in the team, and scored his first goal on December 25, 2010 against Al-Ahly Club in a match that ended in a 1-1 draw. His abilities, high skills, and extreme speed contributed to making him the subject of media attention.

Professional In Europe (2012 - 2017)

In 2012, after his remarkable performance with the Arab Contractors Club, Swiss club Basel completed its contract with Salah in April 2012 in a deal that amounted to two million euros. For the second time, Salah was among several offers, the most serious of which were from the English clubs Chelsea and Liverpool. Contrary to the wishes of all his followers, Salah chose to play for the blue shirt, becoming the first Egyptian to join Chelsea in January 2014. Salah’s conditions in Chelsea were not satisfactory to his ambition or his fans. He was on the bench for long periods that Salah was not accustomed to in his career, so his decision was to leave, and on February 3, 2015, Mohamed Salah moved to the Italian club Fiorentina on loan for a period of six months, with the club having the right to renew it for another season. At the beginning of the 2015-16 football season, specifically on August 6, 2015, Mohamed Salah joined the Italian club Roma on loan from Chelsea for one year, with the Roma club having the right to buy in the future. On August 3, 2016, Roma bought Mohamed Salah from Chelsea for 15. One million euros, and the 2016-17 season was one of Salah’s best seasons, as he was able to score 19 goals per season.

Liverpool (2017 - Now)

On June 22, 2017 - as a result of Salah’s remarkable brilliance in his final season with Roma - the English club Liverpool announced its contract with Mohamed Salah in a historic deal at all levels, as the value of the deal amounted to 42 million euros. This deal made Mohamed Salah the most expensive African player. On July 2, 2018, Salah signed a new long-term contract with Liverpool, and on August 30, 2018, Salah was selected among the “three-man” list of candidates as the best player of the year in UEFA, and he came in third place. Salah became the highest-scoring African player in the history of the English Premier League.

My Achievement

Mohamed Salah Hamed Mahrous Ghaly (born June 15, 1992) is an Egyptian footballer. He plays as a right-winger for Liverpool FC in the English Premier League and the Egyptian national team. He is considered one of the most prominent Arab and African players, as he has won many awards, most notably the Best Player Award in England 2018, the Goal of the Season Award in the English Premier League for the 2017-18 season, the Best African Player Award for 2017 and 2018, and the Best African Player Award by the BBC for 2017.

And 2018, the best player in the English Premier League for the year 2018, and the Golden Boot Award in the English Premier League (top scorer in the English Premier League) in the years 2018, 2019 and 2022. He was chosen by the American Time magazine in 2019 as one of the 100 most influential people in the world.
